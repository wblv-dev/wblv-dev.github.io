About

Who I am.

I've spent five years in cyber security across a range of environments — starting in a two-person SOC handling thousands of alerts a month and ending up leading a team of five, delivering managed detection services, and running a multi-million-pound greenfield CNI programme for a major UK energy operator.

My strongest ground is the intersection of security engineering and operations. I care about understanding how systems are built well enough to defend them properly — not just monitoring them from a distance. The CNI programme brought that into focus: deploying OPNsense HA firewall pairs, CIS Level 2 hardening across Linux and Windows estates, out-of-band management architecture, and full production handover against NCSC CAF and NIST CSF.

Outside of work I run an extensive homelab — a three-node Proxmox/Ceph cluster that mirrors the infrastructure I work with professionally. It's where I test tooling before it goes anywhere near a client environment, and where I keep hands-on depth across things that don't come up in the day-to-day.
